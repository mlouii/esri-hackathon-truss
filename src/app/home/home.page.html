Food Deserts

A food desert is an urban area where it is difficult to buy affordable or good-quality fresh food. A community garden is a great way at addressing this problem. If this is something that is important to you, selecting this filter will ensure only parcels within food deserts will be considered.

Public Transport

An important logistical aspect of creating a community garden is ensuring every community member has reasonable transportation access to the selected site. Enabling this filter will remove any parcels not within a reasonable distance to public transportation routes.

Public Schools

You may find more community member engagement if your selected sight is near a public school. Enabling this filter will make sure only parcels within reasonable proximity to public schools are considered.

Heavy Metals

A very important component of choosing the right soil to grow on is making sure the soil is not contaminated with heavy metals. Heavy metals may affect the quality of plant growth, but more importantly they may cause serious health problems over a long-period of time. Common heavy metals to be wary of include lead (Pb), chromium (Cr), arsenic (As), zinc (Zn), cadmium (Cd), copper (Cu), mercury (Hg), and nickel (Ni). Enabling this filter will attempt to remove parcels from consideration which may have been exposed to heavy metals. However, using heavy metal testing kits are still strongly recommended for each selected site.

SNAP

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is a federal program that provides food-purchasing assistance for low- and no-income people. You may want to use this filter if you wish to target areas with SNAP recipients.

This filter only considers parcels in a low-income tract (50 percent of households with incomes below 60 percent of the Area Median Gross Income)
